Silbiger Y-DNA Project

The Silbiger Y-DNA Project has been developed to see whether or not various branches of the Silbiger family tree are related. To date, DNA testing has determined that 5 branches of Silbigers are related. A simple DNA test determines if participants are descended from a common Silbiger ancestor. The test provides a yes or no answer and is useful for distantly related groups that cannot be connected by birth, marriage, or death records. The project is a work in progress and we welcome new members.

Through a simple cheek swab, we can determine genetic links. The test is painless, appropriate measures are taken to ensure privacy, and we have established a fund so that the test will be free of charge. Participants must be male and they should have the Silbiger surname or spelling variant. Results from each person tested are applicable to all of that person's known male and female Silbiger relatives.

For further information about the Silbiger Y-DNA Project, contact Jeffrey Silbiger. For general information or technical information about DNA testing, look at the Family Tree DNA website.

Silbiger Family Database

The Silbiger Family Database continues the work of my father, Jeffrey Jacob Silbiger, and our cousin Miroslav (Silbiger) Siba in preserving our family's history. This database provides an easy way for us to keep track of our family's stories and connections, ensuring that future generations can enjoy and remember our heritage.
